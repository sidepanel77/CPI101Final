CURIE

Demo and Technical Explanation

Curie is an AR Companion that appears in the real world through your smart device. She will be there to provide support and

positivity whenever you would like it and will send you messages periodically to see how you’re doing. Whenever you open the app

she might be in a variety of poses, from relaxed, to curious, to pensive. In a world that is changing so fast it can be nice to have

someone who can always be there. Through an augmented reality API Curie can appear anywhere you are. We use a normal-vector-based agglomerative

algorithm to detect curved as well as planar surfaces in 3D point clouds to keep Curie on surfaces that make sense, avoiding the jumpy glitches

that can be found with most augmented reality projects.

More than just appearing as if real, Curie works to act as real as possible. Through tracking the pitch and tone of your responses to her and

cross referencing that to data about how these two qualities we will match Curie’s responses to yours. This is in an attempt to keep her at the

most positive she can be. If your device has the capability and you give permission, your facial recognition data will be used to discern your

mood so that Curie can empathize and act accordingly. A sad mood and responses will bring a solemn but supportive response, a cheerful one will get

a hopeful and encouraging response, etc. Later on, we hope to include an algorithm that tracks the trend of the user’s moods which would be used to

augment Curie’s behavior accordingly.
